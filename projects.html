Full-Stack Hotel Booking System (Completed)

Node.js, PostgreSQL, React, CSS | Mar 2025

This comprehensive group project involved building a full-stack hotel booking system designed to meet database requirements. Our team of three began by creating an Entity-Relationship (ER) diagram that modeled hotel chains, hotels, rooms, clients, employees, and bookings, establishing clear relationships and constraints. This diagram was then translated into a relational database schema to ensure the structure was optimized for real-world use.

We carefully defined primary keys, foreign keys and domain constraints to maintain data integrity throughout all operations. Using PostgreSQL with pgAdmin 4, we implemented the schema and enforced these constraints, making sure that our database could accurately represent hotel data, room availability, and customer reservations without inconsistency or errors.

Our database contained detailed data for five hotel chains, each with at least eight hotels spanning multiple categories and geographical zones. We designed the room data to reflect diverse capacities and sizes, ensuring that at least two hotels shared the same zone for meaningful querying. Beyond data insertion, we implemented complex SQL operations including queries and triggers that automatically enforced business rules such as preventing double bookings or invalid updates.

The project culminated with a React-based web application interface that allowed both clients and hotel employees to interact seamlessly with the system. Clients could search for rooms by multiple criteria such as dates, capacity and price with the interface dynamically updating available options. Employees had additional capabilities to manage reservations, convert bookings into active stays, and handle payments.

My favourite part of this project was designing and implementing the database using SQL. Working with pgAdmin 4 provided a clear and tangible way to visualize and manipulate the data we input through the website. Despite the intense one-month timeframe and balancing the demands of five other courses, our team managed to deliver a functional and well-structured application that met the project requirements, earnig a final grade of 97%.

I'm proud of what we accomplished under these constraints, though I recognize there are many areas for improvement, particularly in the front-end design and user experience. This website itself serves as an opportunity for me to revisit front-end development, deepen my understanding, and build on what I learned during this project.
